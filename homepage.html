" WildFire prediction using Machine learning and Spatial Data obtained from MODIS Terra Satellites "


Among the most devastating natural catastrophes that people frequently cause is wildfire. In the US, there are often more than 100,000 wildfires every year, which destroy 4 to 5 million acres of land and result in significant financial damage. Wildfires that occur naturally, however, have a significant impact on the ecology. This research intends to offer understanding of wildfire, its causes, and behavior, which aids in the formulation of helpful recommendations to improve public safety and reduce the financial harm caused by subsequent wildfires. In academia, there has been some empirical work on modeling wildfires, as well as testing and analysis of several machine learning models for forecasting wildfires. The goal of this initiative is to put theoretical knowledge into practice, particularly as it relates to the United States.

The Motivation


Unidentifying the cause of the wildfire is the one of few main problem. So, with the help of the machine learning models, we were able to predict the cause and the risks of wildfire. And also plan for some preventive measures. The second problem is the awareness of the people in the areas prone to wildfire. The project's goal is to create an accessible web app that can educate those without technical backgrounds on how machine learning models may increase public awareness of wildfires and lower dangers in the future. And also provide insightful information from analyzing historical data and utilizing appropriate machine learning techniques to solve above problems.
